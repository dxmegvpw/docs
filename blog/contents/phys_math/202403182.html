 Derivation of Eq.(3.30) from the infinitesimal. (Mar. 18, 2024)

Let us assume we already obtain the infinitesimal form as \[ \left( 1+\frac{i}{2}\omega_{\rho\sigma}S^{\rho\sigma} \right) \gamma^{\mu} \left( 1-\frac{i}{2}\omega_{\alpha\beta}S^{\alpha\beta} \right) = \left( 1-\frac{i}{2}\omega_{\rho\sigma}\mathcal{J}^{\rho\sigma} \right)^{\mu}_{\ \nu} \gamma^{\nu} . \tag{1} \] We know the general form of the transformation is obtained by performing the infinitesimal transformation an infinite number of times. Therefore we want to evaluate \[ \left( 1+\frac{i}{2}\omega_{\rho\sigma}S^{\rho\sigma} \right)^{m} \gamma^{\mu} \left( 1-\frac{i}{2}\omega_{\alpha\beta}S^{\alpha\beta} \right)^{n} \] for sufficiently large \(n\) and will take the limit \(n\rightarrow\infty\). Note that we consider the infinitesimal situation, thus the parameter should be sufficiently small \(\omega_{\rho\sigma}\sim 1/n\). By using the formula for the infinitesimal \((1)\), we find the relation \[ \left( 1+\frac{i}{2}\omega_{\rho\sigma}S^{\rho\sigma} \right)^{m} \gamma^{\mu} \left( 1-\frac{i}{2}\omega_{\alpha\beta}S^{\alpha\beta} \right)^{n} = \left[ \left( 1-\frac{i}{2}\omega_{\rho\sigma}\mathcal{J}^{\rho\sigma} \right)^{n} \right]^{\mu}_{\ \nu} \gamma^{\nu} \tag{2} \] where right-hand-side denotes \[ \left[ \left( 1-\frac{i}{2}\omega_{\rho\sigma}\mathcal{J}^{\rho\sigma} \right)^{n} \right]^{\mu}_{\ \nu} \equiv \left( 1-\frac{i}{2}\omega_{\rho\sigma}\mathcal{J}^{\rho\sigma} \right)^{\mu}_{\ \alpha_{1}} \left( 1-\frac{i}{2}\omega_{\rho\sigma}\mathcal{J}^{\rho\sigma} \right)^{\alpha_{1}}_{\ \alpha_{2}} \cdots \left( 1-\frac{i}{2}\omega_{\rho\sigma}\mathcal{J}^{\rho\sigma} \right)^{\alpha_{n-1}}_{\ \nu} . \] We will take the limit \(n\rightarrow\infty\) at last. When performing it, we should use formula \[ \lim_{\n\rightarrow\infty} \left( 1+\frac{x}{n} \right\)^{n} = \lim_{\n\rightarrow\infty} \left[ \left( 1+\dfrac{1}{\dfrac{n}{x}} \right\)^{n/x} \right]^{x} = e^{x} , \] which holds by the definition of the Napier number. Since the parameter has the order \(1/n\), factors to the \(n\)-th power in (2) goes to the exponential and we finally attain the general form as \[ \exp\left(\frac{i}{2}\omega_{\rho\sigma}S^{\rho\sigma}\right) \gamma^{\mu} \exp\left(-\frac{i}{2}\omega_{\rho\sigma}S^{\rho\sigma}\right) = \exp\left(-\frac{i}{2}\omega_{\rho\sigma}\mathcal{J}^{\rho\sigma}\right)^{\mu}_{\ \nu} \gamma^{\nu} . \]
