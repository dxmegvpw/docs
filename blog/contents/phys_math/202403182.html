 Derivation of Eq.(3.30) from the infinitesimal. (Mar. 18, 2024)

Let us assume we already obtain the infinitesimal form as \[ \left( 1+\frac{i}{2}\omega_{\rho\sigma}S^{\rho\sigma} \right) \gamma^{\mu} \left( 1-\frac{i}{2}\omega_{\alpha\beta}S^{\alpha\beta} \right) = \left( 1-\frac{i}{2}\omega_{\rho\sigma}\mathcal{J}^{\rho\sigma} \right)^{\mu}_{\nu} \gamma^{\nu} . \tag{1} \] We know the general form of the transformation is obtained by performing the infinitesimal transformation an infinite number of times. Therefore we want to evaluate \[ \left( 1+\frac{i}{2}\omega_{\rho\sigma}S^{\rho\sigma} \right)^{m} \gamma^{\mu} \left( 1-\frac{i}{2}\omega_{\alpha\beta}S^{\alpha\beta} \right)^{n} \] for sufficiently large \(n\) and will take the limit \(n\rightarrow\infinity\). Note that we consider the infinitesimal situation, thus the parameter should be sufficiently small \(\omega_{\rho\sigma}\ll 1\). By using the formula for the infinitesimal \((1)\), we find the relation \[ \left( 1+\frac{i}{2}\omega_{\rho\sigma}S^{\rho\sigma} \right)^{m} \gamma^{\mu} \left( 1-\frac{i}{2}\omega_{\alpha\beta}S^{\alpha\beta} \right)^{n} = \left[ \left( 1-\frac{i}{2}\omega_{\rho\sigma}\mathcal{J}^{\rho\sigma} \right)^{n} \right]^{\mu}_{\nu} \gamma^{\nu} \] where right-hand-side denotes \[ \left[ \left( 1-\frac{i}{2}\omega_{\rho\sigma}\mathcal{J}^{\rho\sigma} \right)^{n} \right]^{\mu}_{\nu} \equiv \left( 1-\frac{i}{2}\omega_{\rho\sigma}\mathcal{J}^{\rho\sigma} \right)^{\mu}_{\alpha_{1}} \left( 1-\frac{i}{2}\omega_{\rho\sigma}\mathcal{J}^{\rho\sigma} \right)^{\alpha_{1}}_{\alpha_{2}} \cdots \left( 1-\frac{i}{2}\omega_{\rho\sigma}\mathcal{J}^{\rho\sigma} \right)^{\alpha_{n-1}}_{\nu} . \]
